SARON YITBAREK: Founder of CodeNewbie, the most supportive community of programmers and people learning to code.

Tell us about yourself! Who are you and what do you do?

I’m the founder of CodeNewbie, the most supportive community of programmers and people learning to code. I’m host of the CodeNewbie Podcast and currently organizing Codeland, our first conference. I’m also an organizer for Make A Diff, a community of developers who want to use their tech powers for good.

When you were just learning to code, what made you stick with it?

When things finally worked, it was just enough of a win to keep me going when things were hard and didn’t make any sense. Once I found my own community of coders on the same journey, feeling less alone really helped.

You also make cartoons! How did you get started with that, and what are some of your plans with drawing in the future?

I learned to draw when I was little because my dad was a great artist and I wanted to make the things he could. I never quite got on his level, but I learned enough to tell stories. Drawing’s a tool I use ever so often when it’s the right solution to a problem, but I don’t have any plans with it.

You seems to spend a lot of time online; what are some of your favorite corners of the internet?

I spend most of my time between Twitter, the New York Times, and sometimes Medium. Between the three of those, I usually end up on top of things.
